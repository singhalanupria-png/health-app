# Alternative Ways to Add Photos to Simulator

## Method 1: Drag and Drop Directly (Easiest)

### Step 1: Get a Test Image
1. **Find any image file** on your Mac (.jpg, .png)
2. **Or create a simple test image**

### Step 2: Drag to Simulator
1. **Drag the image** from Finder
2. **Drop it directly onto the Simulator window**
3. **The Photos app should open** automatically
4. **The image is now in Photos!**

## Method 2: Use Photos App Directly

### Step 1: Open Photos App in Simulator
1. **In Simulator**, go to home screen
2. **Find and open the Photos app** (green icon with flower)
3. **Photos app opens**

### Step 2: Drag Image into Photos
1. **Find an image** on your Mac
2. **Drag it from Finder**
3. **Drop it into the Photos app** in Simulator
4. **Image is added!**

## Method 3: Use Safari to Download

### Step 1: Open Safari in Simulator
1. **In Simulator**, open Safari app
2. **Go to any website** with images
3. **Or use a test image URL**

### Step 2: Download Image
1. **Long press on an image**
2. **Select "Add to Photos"** or "Save Image"
3. **Image is saved to Photos**

## Method 4: Use Files App

### Step 1: Add File to Files App
1. **In Simulator**, open Files app
2. **Drag a PDF or image** from Finder into Files app
3. **File is now in Files app**

### Step 2: Test in Your App
1. **Open your HealthReportApp**
2. **Tap "Choose from Files"**
3. **Select the file**

## Quick Test: Drag and Drop

**Simplest method:**
1. **Find any .jpg or .png** on your Mac
2. **Drag it onto the Simulator window**
3. **Photos app opens** and image is added
4. **Open your app** and tap "Choose from Library"
5. **Select the image you just added**

## Troubleshooting

### Drag and Drop Doesn't Work
- Make sure Simulator window is active
- Try dropping on different parts of the Simulator
- Try a different image file

### Photos App is Empty
- Try dragging multiple images
- Or use the Files app method instead

## Recommended: Drag and Drop

**Just drag an image from Finder onto the Simulator - it's the easiest!**
